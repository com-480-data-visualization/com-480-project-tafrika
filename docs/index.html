Let's take a closer look at what you can discover with our powerful tools.

Spatial Visualization

Step onto the court with our interactive scatter map, where you can visualize every shot taken by a player. This tool places you right in the action, showing you the precise locations and outcomes of shots on a basketball court diagram. Imagine exploring the shooting patterns of stars like Stephen Curry or LeBron James, seeing where they hit their sweet spots and where they struggle.

How to Use It:
  • Select the season, team, and player to visualize their shot data.
  • Apply filters to focus on specific shot types or areas.
  • For each player, you get a player card that displays player details, number of shots, and their accuracy for the corresponding area.
Insights You Can Gain:
  • Identify shooting hotspots and cold zones for individual players.
  • Develop targeted training drills and defensive strategies based on player tendencies.

Player Comparative Analysis

Dive into the heart of player performance with our comparative analysis scatter plot of the season 2014-15. This visualization lets you compare players across a wide range of offensive and defensive metrics, providing a clear picture of who dominates the court. Picture yourself evaluating the scoring efficiency of guards like James Harden against the defensive prowess of forwards like Kawhi Leonard, all in one dynamic chart.

How to Use It:
  • Choose two metrics from the dropdown menus to plot on the x-axis and y-axis. These can be offensive (e.g., points per game) or defensive (e.g., opponent field goal percentage).
  • For example, select "Points per Game" for the x-axis and "Three Pointers Made per Game" for the y-axis to find top scorers who excel at three-point shooting.
  • Use the dropdown menu to categorize players by attributes like team or position. Each dot will be color-coded based on the selected category.
  • Filter the data to focus on specific groups. For instance, choose "Houston Rockets" to compare only the players on that team.
  • Hover over any dot to see detailed information about the player, including their name, team, and statistics for the selected metrics.
Insights You Can Gain:
  • Discover players who excel both offensively and defensively. For example, find those who score efficiently while also limiting opponents' shooting percentages.
  • Analyze metrics like "Shot Accuracy" and "Dribbles Before Shot" to distinguish between isolation players and catch-and-shoot specialists, gaining insights into their playing styles.
  • Easily compare players within a team or position group. This can help in understanding the strengths and weaknesses of different players and making informed decisions about player roles and strategies.
  • Use the comparative analysis to develop game plans. Identify which players are best suited for specific situations, such as needing a reliable scorer or a strong defender in clutch moments.

Quarterly Performance

Experience the game in real-time with our quarter-by-quarter performance ranking graph. This visualization tracks player performance across each quarter, revealing who shines in clutch moments and who maintains consistency throughout the game. Imagine analyzing the fourth-quarter heroics of players like Kyrie Irving or the steady contributions of a star like Giannis Antetokounmpo.

How to Use It:
  • Choose the performance metric you want to analyze from the dropdown menu, such as points per quarter, field goal percentage (FG%), or three-pointers made.
  • Use the dropdown menus to select the season, and player. For instance, choose the 2022-23 season and Kyrie Irving to see his quarter-by-quarter stats.
  • Each line on the graph represents a player's performance across the four quarters. Click on a line to highlight it and display the player's detailed statistics on the right. The player card provides key details such as points per quarter, attempted three-pointers per quarter, field goal percentage, and more.
  • Filter the graph by team or position to focus on specific groups. For example, selecting "Dallas Mavericks" will show only the players from that team, making it easier to compare their performances.
Insights You Can Gain:
  • Identify players who excel in the fourth quarter or other critical moments. See who steps up when it matters most and can be relied upon in tight games.
  • Discover players who consistently perform well across all quarters, providing steady contributions throughout the game.
  • Use the data to inform coaching decisions, such as which players to keep on the court during crucial periods or how to adjust rotations to maximize performance.
